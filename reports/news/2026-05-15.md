<b>═ 🔥 TOP STORY ═</b>
Espressif Systems has published the official ESP32-S31 product page and full technical specification, confirming a dual-core 320 MHz RISC-V SoC with a 128-bit SIMD data path, hardware MMU, Wi-Fi 6 (802.11ax), Bluetooth 5.4 (LE + Classic), IEEE 802.15.4 (Thread + Zigbee), and a 1000 Mbps Gigabit Ethernet MAC — the first Espressif SoC to bundle all five connectivity standards simultaneously in one die. The hardware MMU and wider data path make bare-metal RTOS ports and lightweight Linux deployments more tractable; 512 KB on-chip SRAM plus support for 250 MHz 8-bit DDR PSRAM address the memory ceiling that limited ESP32-S3 camera and inference workloads. OEM samples are in lead-customer hands now; retail development boards are expected late 2026 with full ESP-IDF, ESP-Matter, and ESP-GMF software support from day one. For engineers working with the ESP32-S3 today, the S31 is the natural upgrade path — same vendor toolchain, radically expanded connectivity, and RISC-V cores that will be better supported by Zephyr's growing RISC-V backend.
