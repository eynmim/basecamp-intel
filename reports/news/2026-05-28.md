<b>═ 🔥 TOP STORY ═</b>
wolfSSL CVE-2026-5194 (CVSS 9.3) — "Forged Trust" — is a critical signature-verification bypass in all wolfSSL releases prior to 5.9.1 allowing attackers to present X.509 certificates that pass ECDSA, DSA, ML-DSA, Ed25519, and Ed448 validation without a legitimate private key. The root cause is missing hash-digest-size and OID checks in the signature path: a crafted certificate with an undersized digest clears validation, enabling unauthenticated TLS/DTLS man-in-the-middle and full device impersonation across any certificate-based mTLS session. The vulnerability affects an estimated 5 billion devices — IoT sensors, routers, industrial controllers, military systems — wherever wolfSSL provides TLS, including FreeRTOS+TLS integrations and ESP-IDF versions prior to v6.0. wolfSSL 5.9.1, released April 8, 2026, is the fix; any firmware performing mutual certificate authentication must audit its wolfSSL version and update. ESP-IDF v6.0 replaced wolfSSL with MbedTLS 4.x + PSA Crypto, removing the exposure on that branch entirely.
