<b>═ 🔥 TOP STORY ═</b>
The Bluetooth SIG formally adopted Core Specification 6.3 on May 6, 2026, marking the second release under the new bi-annual cadence. The headline feature is Channel Sounding per-PHY RTT accuracy: devices can now report Round-Trip-Time accuracy separately for each PHY (1M, 2M, Coded), enabling smarter PHY selection for centimeter-level ranging in multi-PHY deployments. The spec also enlarges the HCI Supported Commands bitmask and LE Event Mask for future scalability, and aligns RF requirements between Classic BR/EDR and LE to enable more power-efficient dual-mode radio architectures. For BLE firmware engineers already using Channel Sounding (introduced in BT 6.0), 6.3 changes accuracy reporting semantics — controller and host stack updates from Nordic, Espressif, and Silicon Labs are expected in their next major SDK drops. This is the spec to benchmark your ranging firmware against before taping out any BLE 6.x sensor tag or asset-tracking product.
