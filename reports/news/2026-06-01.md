<b>═ 🔥 TOP STORY ═</b>
The Linux kernel "Dirty Frag" vulnerability pair (CVE-2026-43284, CVE-2026-43500) disclosed May 7 continues to threaten embedded Linux devices, with CVE-2026-43500 still unpatched across all major distributions as of today. CVE-2026-43284 (CVSS 8.8) exploits a flaw in the kernel's ESP (IPsec Encapsulating Security Protocol) module; CVE-2026-43500 (CVSS 7.8) targets the RxRPC kernel module — chained together they provide a reliable local privilege escalation from any unprivileged process to root. A public proof-of-concept exploit was released before patches were complete, meaning every OpenWRT-based gateway, Buildroot/Yocto edge node, and embedded Linux hub in the field with these modules loaded is currently rootable. For embedded engineers the threat model extends beyond servers: any IoT gateway or industrial edge device running a stock Linux kernel with ESP or RxRPC available is fully compromised by a single unprivileged process. Patch CVE-2026-43284 in your BSP immediately; explicitly blacklist rxrpc from your kernel config as the CVE-2026-43500 mitigation until upstream produces a fix.
