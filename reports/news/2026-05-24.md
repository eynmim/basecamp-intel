<b>═ 🔥 TOP STORY ═</b>
Espressif published official hardware documentation for two ESP32-S31 development boards on May 22, marking the first concrete developer collateral for the chip since its March announcement. The ESP32-S31-Function-CoreBoard-1 pairs the WROOM-3 module with Gigabit Ethernet and USB 2.0 OTG; the ESP32-S31-Korvo-1 targets multimedia with a dual-microphone array, 4.3" LCD, 3 MP camera, two speaker connectors, and a microSD slot. The S31 is the most significant ESP32 generational leap since the S3: it swaps Xtensa for dual-core RISC-V, adds Wi-Fi 6, BLE 5.4, native 802.15.4 (Thread/Zigbee), and on-die Gigabit Ethernet, all in a single SoC. Both boards require ESP-IDF master branch today; stable SDK support is pending but the documentation drop signals silicon availability is close. If you are planning any new ESP32-S3 design in the next six months, prototype it — but seriously evaluate whether to target S31 instead for the longer hardware lifecycle.
