<b>═ 🔥 TOP STORY ═</b>
A public proof-of-concept (PoC) exploit for CVE-2026-55200 — the CVSS 9.2 pre-auth heap overflow in libssh2 — was released on June 25, moving this vulnerability from "theoretical critical risk" to "actively weaponised." The flaw lives in <i>ssh2_transport_read()</i> in transport.c: an attacker-controlled <i>packet_length</i> field undergoes a 32-bit integer wrap, underallocating the heap buffer, then subsequent code writes past the allocation boundary using the original large value. No authentication is required — the exploit fires during initial transport negotiation. The PoC, published in the exploitarium repository, packages a C11 driver that demonstrates the integer-wrap path in under 50 lines and works against any libssh2 version through 1.11.1. Still no official libssh2 1.11.2 release exists; the only available fix is upstream commit 97acf3d, which means every embedded vendor that statically links the library must independently cherry-pick the patch, rebuild, re-sign, and push firmware. IoT devices, NAS boxes, industrial controllers, and OTA update daemons — most on multi-month patch cycles with no auto-update mechanism — remain exposed indefinitely. This is the moment to audit your dependency tree.

IEC published the revised IEC 62443-4-2 in April 2026. Two new mandatory controls for industrial IoT gateways and cloud-connected PLC communication modules: authenticated firmware signing, and cryptographically verified OTA update mechanisms.

Previously, IEC 62443-4-2 addressed industrial component security at a level that left some IIoT gateway and cloud-PLC categories partially outside its scope. The 2026 revision closes that gap explicitly.

If you are building or certifying an industrial gateway or field device controller, the component certification scope just expanded.

Firmware signing and secure OTA are engineering work that takes months to implement correctly, not days. Bootloader architecture decisions, key management hardware, signing pipeline, rollback protection, and update transport security all need to be in place before the compliance review.
