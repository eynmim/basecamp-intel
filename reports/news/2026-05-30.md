<b>═ 🔥 TOP STORY ═</b>
Three independent chipset-level wireless vulnerabilities landed in NVD and vendor bulletins within a 48-hour window ending May 29. The Qualcomm May 2026 Security Bulletin disclosed CVE-2026-25254 (CVSS 9.8) — an improper-authorization flaw in Qualcomm's SocketIO interface enabling unauthenticated remote code execution across Snapdragon 8 Elite, FastConnect 7800, and Snapdragon Auto 5G platforms — alongside CVE-2026-25293 (CVSS 9.6), a buffer overflow in powerline communication firmware exploitable by adjacent network attackers without credentials. Independently, the Linux kernel's Broadcom b43 driver (CVE-2026-46122) and MediaTek MT7921 mt76 driver (CVE-2026-46136) both received NVD entries on May 28 for an OOB read and a CLC buffer length underflow respectively. All four vulnerabilities affect hardware commonly deployed in IoT gateways, industrial wireless nodes, and embedded Linux compute modules — a cluster of wireless-layer attacks this dense in one week is rare and warrants an immediate audit of any gateway running unpatched Linux kernels or Qualcomm-based wireless SoCs.

• <b>EU CRA Vulnerability Reporting Mandate — Sept 11, 2026 (103 Days)</b> [High] — Starting September 11, 2026, manufacturers of any product with digital elements sold in the EU must report actively exploited vulnerabilities to ENISA within 24 hours (early warning) and 72 hours (full technical notification); SBOM in machine-readable format and coordinated vulnerability disclosure processes are mandatory at the same date. Non-compliance penalties: up to €15M or 2.5% of global annual turnover. Any IoT product shipping to the EU needs an operational PSIRT pipeline before this date. <a href="https://securityboulevard.com/2026/05/the-eu-cyber-resilience-act-a-complete-compliance-guide-for-2026-and-beyond/">Source →</a>
• <b>CRA SBOM Requirement — Machine-Readable Format Mandatory for Every Embedded Product</b> [Medium] — The CRA mandates a machine-readable Software Bill of Materials (SBOM) covering all software components, including RTOS, middleware, and third-party libraries embedded in firmware. For ESP32/STM32 products, this means generating and maintaining a CycloneDX or SPDX SBOM that tracks every ESP-IDF component version, FreeRTOS library, and vendor HAL library used in production firmware — a new CI/CD gate requirement. <a href="https://wizzdev.com/blog/cyber-resilience-act-2026-guide-for-iot-devices/">Source →</a>
