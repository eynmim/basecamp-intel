<b>1. Post: [Security wake-up] — 18 vulnerabilities. One library. In firmware you shipped last month.</b>
cURL 8.21.0 landed June 27 with a record 18 CVEs patched in a single release — the most ever in one cURL update.

cURL is not a command-line tool. It is the HTTP client library embedded in over 20 billion active devices across smartphones, cars, smart TVs, industrial gateways, and IoT firmware stacks. If your firmware makes an HTTP request, there is a good chance it routes through libcurl.

The problem for embedded engineers: firmware has no auto-update mechanism for bundled libraries. Patching libcurl means a firmware rebuild, OTA campaign, and waiting for every device in the field to pull the update. In the meantime, the vulnerability details are public.

The 18 CVEs include integer overflow in URL parsing and heap buffer overflow in cookie handling — both reachable by a server responding to your firmware's outbound HTTP request. If your OTA download endpoint or telemetry pipeline is compromised, these fire on the device.

Identify what libcurl version is in your current firmware builds and when your next rebuild is scheduled. That gap is your live exposure window.
