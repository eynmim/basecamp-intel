<b>3. Post: Security wake-up — the pairing flow is the attack surface</b>
The bug isn't in Zephyr's Bluetooth stack in general. It's specifically in the handshake that's supposed to be routine: broadcast-audio pairing.

CVE-2026-10660 is a GATT buffer overflow in Zephyr's Bluetooth Broadcast Assistant — the role a device plays when it's helping another device find and join a broadcast audio stream (Auracast-style). A malicious peer can trigger it during that exact exchange.

The pattern worth internalizing: profile-specific GATT handlers are exactly the kind of narrow, less-tested code path that slips through general BLE stack fuzzing. If you're running Zephyr's Bluetooth stack instead of NimBLE and have Broadcast Assistant compiled in, it's worth the ten minutes to check your patch level.

Does your BLE threat model account for the profile-specific handlers, or just the pairing flow itself?
