<b>═ 🔥 TOP STORY ═</b>
Espressif's ESP32-S31 moved from paper launch to purchasable hardware on May 22 with two official dev boards: the CoreBoard-1 ($19.70) based on the ESP32-S31-WROOM-3 module, and the multimedia-focused Korvo-1 ($58.41) adding a dual-microphone array, 4.3-inch LCD, and 3MP camera. The SoC underneath is the most feature-rich Espressif chip yet — dual RISC-V cores at 320 MHz with a 128-bit SIMD data path, 512 KB SRAM, Gigabit Ethernet MAC, Wi-Fi 6, Bluetooth 5.4 (LE + Classic), and an IEEE 802.15.4 radio for simultaneous Thread and Zigbee — all on a single chip with PUF-based secure key management. For engineers already on ESP32-S3 projects, the arrival of reference dev boards is the practical trigger to evaluate IDF 5.x→6.x migration effort and assess where the S31's GbE backhaul changes the gateway architecture equation.
