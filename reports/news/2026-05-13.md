<b>═ 🔥 TOP STORY ═</b>
SiFive launched the Performance P570 Gen 3 RISC-V processor IP on May 12, 2026 — a 3-wide, 13-stage out-of-order superscalar core compliant with the RVA23 ISA profile, scalable to 16 cores, and equipped with a 128-bit vector engine supporting FP16 and BF16. Against the prior P550 Gen 1 the core delivers a 13% performance uplift, 13% lower dynamic power, and — critically for edge AI — up to 21× acceleration of AI workloads at the same process node, driven by the upgraded vector engine and the mandatory RVA23 V extension. The RVA23 profile has formal backing from Google, Red Hat, and Canonical, meaning Android and enterprise Linux are first-class targets; SiFive ships a companion gen-2 IOMMU, WorldGuard security IP, and a RISC-V AIA interrupt controller as a complete SoC fabric. This marks RISC-V crossing from "interesting alternative" to "credible primary for premium AIoT and edge SoC tapeouts," with every SoC house evaluating P570 Gen 3 alongside Arm Cortex-A55/A78AE for 2027 designs.
