<b>═ 🔥 TOP STORY ═</b>
The EU Cyber Resilience Act (CRA) mandatory vulnerability-reporting deadline is exactly four months away: September 11, 2026. From that date, every manufacturer of "products with digital elements"—including ESP32-based IoT devices, industrial sensors, and consumer firmware—must report actively exploited vulnerabilities to ENISA and national CSIRTs within 24 hours, even for legacy products already shipped. Full enforcement including security-by-design requirements and conformance testing follows December 11, 2027. Espressif published a dedicated CRA compliance guide for ESP32 developers in March 2026, and ETSI EN 303 645 now functions as the harmonised standard bridging CRA baseline requirements. Any embedded team still without a Software Bill of Materials (SBOM) and vulnerability-management pipeline is out of time.
